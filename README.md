# Descriptive Analytics Dashboard — LEGO Investment & Pricing Trends

An Excel-based descriptive analytics project analyzing 2,322 LEGO sets released between 1981 and 2014 to uncover pricing patterns, investment premiums, and market behavior across themes and time periods.

---

## Overview

LEGO sets have become a recognized alternative investment vehicle, with retired sets frequently appreciating well above their original retail price on the secondary market. This project applies descriptive analytics to a historical dataset of LEGO sets to quantify that behavior — examining how price, piece count, theme, and year of release relate to secondary market performance.

The deliverable is an interactive Excel dashboard designed to surface actionable business insights for collectors, analysts, and anyone tracking the LEGO resale market.

---

## What's Inside

The workbook (`descriptiveanalytics.xlsx`) is organized across multiple analytical layers:

**Raw Data & Cleaning** — 2,322 LEGO sets with attributes including theme, year, piece count, retail price (USD), and secondary market value.

**Statistical Summary** — descriptive statistics covering mean, median, mode, standard deviation, min, max, and range across key numerical fields. Highlights distribution shape and data spread at a glance.

**Pricing Trend Analysis** — how average retail prices evolved across the 1981–2014 period, segmented by era and theme category.

**Investment Premium Analysis** — calculated premium values (secondary market price vs. original retail) to identify which themes and set characteristics correlate with the highest returns after retirement.

**Theme-Wise Breakdown** — comparative analysis across LEGO themes to determine which product lines commanded the highest prices, largest piece counts, and strongest investment returns.

**Interactive Dashboard** — pivot-table-powered charts and slicers enabling dynamic filtering by theme, year range, and price tier.

---

## Key Questions Explored

- Which LEGO themes generate the highest investment premiums post-retirement?
- How has average retail price per piece changed over the decades?
- Is there a relationship between piece count and secondary market value?
- Which years or product eras produced the most valuable sets?
- What does the distribution of set prices look like — are there outliers driving the averages?

---

## Tools Used

Microsoft Excel — PivotTables, PivotCharts, slicers, conditional formatting, statistical functions, and formula-driven summary sheets.

---

## Dataset

The dataset covers officially released LEGO sets from 1981 to 2014, including retail pricing and resale market data. It contains 2,322 records across multiple themes spanning licensed and original LEGO product lines.
